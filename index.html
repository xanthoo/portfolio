  • 2nd phase of Client project - Brainstorming final idea

    Coming up with the idea

    This was one of the most challening phase of any project for me personally, because we had a lot of freedom to come up with a suitable idea. However, this proved to be easier said than done, since we needed to satisfy learning outcomes, client "restricitons", and also follow the IoB and IoT frameworks.
    During the brainstorming sessions we came up with a Miro board where we all gave our potential ideas and we ultimately chose my idea of sleep behavior.

    Value proposition:


    Why did we choose sleep?

    We believe that sleep is one of the most important behaviors of man kind. Lack of sleep has numerous negative effects on humans, such as: low immunity, bad mental wellbeing, higher chance of heart disease, worse memory and much more.

    We also believe that people do not get enough sleep, and struggle to keep being productive throughout the day, thus why we are going to try and help with sleep deprivation by using IoB and targetting bad sleeping habbits. In some cases the lack of productivity can prove to be fatal in some professions, such as surgeons, pilots, cruise ship captains, ect.


    What if we all had enough sleep?

    The benefits of good sleep are too many to list, but a great refrence is this netflix series. Some benefits are: getting sick less often, lowering your risk of diabetes or heart problems, reducing stress and improving overall mood, thinking more clearly and performing better at the workplace and/or school. I strongly believe that during these troubled times, with the pandemic still being prevalent in our lives, sleep is the most obvious and crutial boost to our overall health, but people seem to neglect it.


    Why do we sleep less?

    Sleep deprivation is often driven by voluntary choices that reduce available sleep time. For example, a person who decides to stay up late to binge-watch a TV series may experience sleep deprivation. An inconsistent sleep schedule may facilitate these decisions and make them feel less intentional in the moment.
    Work obligations are another common contributor to sleep deprivation. People who work multiple jobs or extended hours may not have enough time for quality sleep.

  • 3rd phase of Client project - Technical challenges, innovation and descisions

    Which technologies we will use, and why them?

    After deliberation, both internal within the group, with the client and with the teachers, we decided to go with the Ionic framework, in order to create a mobile application that both iPhone and Android users alike can use. This also explains why we are not using Swift (iOS programming language), Java or Kotlin(Android).

    For gathering the data needed for the sleeping cycle we will be using the FitBit SDK and it's provided open source API. We are going to most likely use Angular's Http Client Module to communicate with the API, however we are also looking into using React's axios. The reason we are unsure what to use just yet is because we are comparing the pros and cons of both frameworks.
    [Comparison and analysis]


    How are we planning to be innovative?

    We want to tap into specifics when it comes to proper,deep and healthy sleep. We will analyze sleeping periods, as well as quality of the sleep by using the above mentioned FitBit API communication techniques. After gathering the information we will store it accordingly, most likely in a DB. After that we will provide targeted tips to the user.
    We also plan on forcing the user to stop doing something we deem dangerous, such as driving with, let's say less than 4 hours of sleep. We will detect the acceleration using the sensors in the phone, and activate a vibration that could only be turned off if the acceleration goes under 30 km/h.
    Other methods could be using extreme examples of why sleeping X amount of hours is bad for your health (such as extreme real life events that occurred due to lack of sleep).
